# Understoria — Code of Conduct

> **Status:** founding draft. Per the Agent 5 acceptance criteria, this
> document will be signed by all founding members and reviewed annually.
> It is intended to be a living document, amended by community process.

---

## Our commitments

Understoria exists to help communities practice mutual aid and build
collective power. We are bound together not by a platform but by the
work we do for each other. This Code of Conduct describes how we treat
each other while we do that work.

We commit to:

1. **Solidarity, not charity.** We show up for each other as equals.
   One hour of help equals one hour of help. No one is above needing
   help; no one earns their way into deserving care.

2. **Horizontal structure.** No permanent positions, no hidden
   hierarchy. Roles rotate. Power is distributed on purpose. When
   someone is doing coordinating work, we name that explicitly so it
   can be shared, accounted for, and handed off.

3. **Collective care.** We care about each other's wellbeing beyond
   the transaction. If someone is burning out, we notice. If someone
   keeps giving and never receives, we check in.

4. **Respect for privacy.** We do not screenshot, forward, or copy
   platform content out of the community. We do not disclose
   membership, activity, or identity to outsiders without consent. We
   honor pseudonyms.

5. **Honest accounting.** We confirm exchanges that actually happened.
   We do not inflate hours. We do not game the credit system. If we
   get things wrong, we say so.

6. **Direct communication.** We talk to each other, not about each
   other. When conflict arises we seek to understand before we seek
   to be understood.

7. **Anti-oppression.** No racism, sexism, homophobia, transphobia,
   ableism, xenophobia, casteism, anti-Semitism, Islamophobia, or
   other forms of discrimination. No jokes that punch down. No "just
   asking questions" that corrode the safety of the people we are
   supposed to be in community with.

8. **No exploitation.** We do not use the platform to surveil,
   recruit for commercial purposes, extract data, or undermine the
   organizing work of any community — including our own.

## What we ask of each other

- **Show up.** If you claim a post, follow through or let the other
  person know early.
- **Ask for what you need.** Asking is never a failure. Seed credits
  exist because asking before giving is a normal part of being in
  community.
- **Offer what you can, honestly.** Do not overcommit because you
  feel you should.
- **Check in with new members.** The first month is the hardest.
- **Question metrics that encourage the wrong behavior.** We review
  our own systems regularly and are willing to change them.

## Concrete boundaries

These are not aspirations; they are the line.

- **No discussion of organizing on employer-owned devices, networks,
  or accounts.** Not ours, not anyone's.
- **No sharing of membership information outside the community.** Not
  in screenshots, not in casual conversation, not with partners or
  family who are not members.
- **No coercion of participation.** Nobody has to use Understoria.
  Nobody has to respond to every need. "No" is a complete answer.
- **No retaliation** for good-faith criticism, reports of harm, or
  requests for mediation.
- **No commercial scraping, data sale, or resale.** The license is
  AGPL, the spirit is cooperative. If you run a node, you do so in
  service of a community, not a business built on top of it.

## When things go wrong

Conflict happens. Harm happens. We handle it without a punitive
reflex.

1. **Direct conversation** — most things resolve here. Go to the
   person. Say what happened and how it landed.

2. **Mediated conversation** — if direct conversation isn't possible
   or didn't work, request a mediator. Mediators are rotating community
   members who facilitate, not judge.

3. **Community circle** — for serious conflicts or repeat harm, the
   moderation committee (three rotating members) facilitates a
   structured restorative process.

4. **Suspension or removal** — in cases of serious, repeated, or
   unwilling-to-change behavior. Requires committee consensus. Appeal
   is available to the full community.

5. **Appeals** — any enforcement action can be appealed. Appeals go
   to a different set of community members than the original decision.

No single person can unilaterally ban, suspend, or silence anyone.

## Specifically about this software

Understoria is open-source and federated. You can run a node and fork
the code without our permission. But if you run Understoria for a
community:

- **Your community adopts its own Code of Conduct.** Ours is a
  starting point, not a final document. Ratify one that fits.
- **Honor the design philosophy.** No skill-based credit multipliers,
  no leaderboards, no algorithmic ranking of members. These are
  non-negotiable for use of the Understoria name and branding.
- **Protect your members.** Follow the Threat Model and Opsec Guide.
  Run the software that was designed to keep them safe; do not rip
  out the parts that do that work.

## Amendments and review

This document is reviewed once a year by the full community.
Amendments are proposed via the governance process and require
modified consensus to adopt.
