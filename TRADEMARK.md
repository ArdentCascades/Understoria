# Trademark Policy

The name **Understoria**, the forest-canopy logo, and the phrase
"Built with solidarity, not surveillance" are trademarks of the
Understoria community. The source code is licensed under
AGPL-3.0-or-later; the name and brand are not.

This separation is intentional. It prevents a bad-faith fork from
deploying a modified version under our name and eroding the trust
members have in what "Understoria" means.

---

## What you can always do

- **Fork the code.** Under AGPL, freely.
- **Run a modified version for your own community.** Call it
  something else. You can say it's "based on Understoria" or
  "a fork of Understoria."
- **Reference Understoria by name** in articles, talks, research
  papers, and discussions. Trademark law doesn't (and shouldn't)
  limit nominative use.
- **Use screenshots** in educational material.

## What requires permission

- **Releasing software under the "Understoria" name** — whether
  modified or unmodified.
- **Using the logo or a confusingly similar logo** on promotional
  materials, websites, or merchandise.
- **Presenting yourself as "an official Understoria project"** or
  implying endorsement.
- **Commercial use of the brand** (selling services branded as
  Understoria, hosting a paid "Understoria instance," etc.).

To request permission, open a discussion on GitHub or contact the
maintainers.

## What we will always refuse

- Use of the name or brand for software that **removes the
  privacy-preserving design** — tracking members, logging IPs,
  storing plaintext personal data, integrating surveillance SDKs.
- Use of the name or brand for **surveillance, union-busting, or
  anti-organizing tooling**, regardless of technical details.
- **Exclusive-use claims.** We will not sign over the name to any
  individual, company, or government.

## Enforcement

We prefer conversations to lawyers. If you're using the name in a
way this policy doesn't cover and you think your use is reasonable,
ask. If we disagree, we'll say so and suggest alternatives.

In cases of clear bad-faith use — typosquatting, impersonation,
fraudulent projects using the brand to attract members — we reserve
the right to take legal action. The point is to protect the
communities who rely on the name.

## The spirit

This policy exists because we've watched enough movement projects
get hijacked by their own popularity. The code is a commons; the
name is a trust anchor. Both should outlast the people who started
them.
